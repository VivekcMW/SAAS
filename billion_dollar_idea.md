## World-Class AI Features to Build

### Tier 1 — Core Moat (Build First)

---

#### 1. AI Match Engine — "SaaS Dating App"

Replace keyword search with intent-based matching.

```
User answers 5 questions:
  - Company size, industry, budget
  - Current tools in stack
  - Primary pain point
  - Team technical level

AI returns ranked top 5 tools with reasoning:
  "Based on your fintech context and existing Salesforce stack,
   HubSpot scores 94% — here is why vs. Pipedrive at 78%..."
```

Users who get matched buy at **3x the rate** of users who browse.

---

#### 2. AI Review Synthesis Engine

Nobody reads 500 reviews. AI reads them for you.

Instead of star ratings, surface:

- **The Consensus** — what 90% of users agree on
- **Power User View** — filtered by verified heavy users
- **Deal-Breakers** — the top recurring complaints
- **Best For / Worst For** — auto-segmented by company size and industry
- **Sentiment Trend** — is the product getting better or worse over the last 6 months?

This turns your review data into a **proprietary AI data product** no competitor can replicate.

---

#### 3. Conversational Discovery (AI Copilot)

A chat interface on every page. Not a FAQ bot — a buying advisor.

```
Buyer: "I need project management for a 40-person engineering team,
        we use Jira today but engineers hate it, budget $2K/month"

AI:    "I hear you — Jira fatigue is real at that scale. Before I recommend,
        two quick questions: are your engineers more frontend or full-stack?
        And do you need Confluence-level docs or just task tracking?
        [Based on your answers, I am ranking Linear, Height, and Plane for you...]"
```

Every conversation is a **lead signal you sell to vendors.** It is also the feature that makes buyers return.

---

#### 4. Buyer Intent Intelligence (Sold to Vendors)

Every action on the platform becomes a signal:

| Action | Signal Strength |
|--------|----------------|
| Viewed vendor profile | Warm |
| Compared vendor to a competitor | Hot |
| Opened pricing page | Purchase-proximate |
| Bookmarked / saved | Shortlisted |

Package as **real-time alerts to vendor sales teams:**
> "A Director of Engineering at a 200-person fintech in Singapore just compared you to Notion for 8 minutes."

This is G2's highest-margin product. **$2K–$10K/month per vendor.**

---

### Tier 2 — Differentiation (Build Next)

---

#### 5. Stack Intelligence — "Your SaaS X-Ray"

Let buyers connect their existing stack. The platform then:

- Detects overlapping tools ("You are paying for Asana AND Monday — here is the overlap")
- Shows underused tools by category benchmark ("Companies your size use 3 analytics tools, you have 7")
- Suggests replacements with ROI projections
- Alerts on renewal dates with negotiation benchmarks

This is what Zylo charges enterprises **$50K–$200K/year** for. Moonmart delivers it as a free-to-buyer feature, funded by vendor leads.

---

#### 6. AI-Powered Vendor Intelligence Dashboard

Give vendors a Bloomberg Terminal for their category:

- **Competitive Sentiment Map** — how your reviews compare to competitors, sentence by sentence
- **Win/Loss Analysis** — when buyers compare you and choose a competitor, show what tipped them
- **Buyer Persona Intelligence** — who visits your profile (role, company size, industry)
- **Content Gap Detector** — "Buyers ask about GDPR compliance — you have not answered this on your profile"
- **Pricing Intelligence** — anonymized data on what buyers in your segment pay for competitors
- **Review Response AI** — draft personalized responses to reviews in one click

Vendors see this and immediately understand it is worth $500–$2K/month.

---

#### 7. Negotiation Intelligence

Hidden killer feature. Almost nobody offers this:

- "Companies your size typically pay 25% less than list price for this tool"
- "This vendor historically offers 30% discounts in Q4"
- "3 companies in your industry negotiated free migration and 2 months free — here is how"
- AI generates a negotiation brief for enterprise buyers

Source data from: community submissions, public pricing intelligence, partner data agreements.

---

#### 8. AI Verification Engine (Trust Moat)

G2 and Capterra's biggest vulnerability is **fake reviews.** Build a trust system:

- **Verified Usage Badge** — linked to actual product login via OAuth, not just email
- **AI Authenticity Score** — LLM analysis of review language patterns, reviewer history, timing clusters
- **Verified Outcome Reviews** — user submits before/after metrics ("reduced onboarding time by 40%"), AI validates coherence
- **Anti-Astroturfing Detection** — flags sudden review spikes, IP clustering, language similarity

Position this as **"The most trusted reviews on the internet."** This alone differentiates from G2.

---

#### 9. AI Briefing Generator (One-Click Procurement Doc)

Procurement teams spend weeks writing software evaluation briefs. Do it in 30 seconds:

User selects 3 tools → clicks "Generate Evaluation Brief" → AI produces:

- Executive summary
- Feature comparison matrix
- Pricing analysis with TCO estimate
- Risk assessment (uptime history, support quality, data residency)
- Recommended vendor with justification
- Appendix of curated top reviews

This is **procurement automation.** Share as PDF or Google Doc. Every share is a viral loop for Moonmart.

---

#### 10. Events + Community Intelligence

The events schema is already in the database. Build on it:

- AI-curated weekly digest: "5 things that changed in your software stack this week"
- Product update alerts ("Notion just released AI templates — here is what that means for your evaluation")
- Community Q&A with AI-synthesized answers from existing review content
- Vendor AMAs (Ask Me Anything) — drives engagement and fresh content
